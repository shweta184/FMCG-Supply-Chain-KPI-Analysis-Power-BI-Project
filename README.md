# 🏭 FMCG Supply Chain KPI Analysis – Power BI Project

This Power BI dashboard analyzes key supply chain performance indicators (KPIs) for AtliQ Mart, a growing FMCG manufacturer. The objective is to monitor and improve logistics performance across cities, customers, and months using actionable insights.

The dashboard tracks On-Time (OT%), In-Full (IF%), and On-Time In-Full (OTIF%) metrics, helping business stakeholders quickly identify areas that need operational improvement.

---

## 🧠 Business Problem

AtliQ Mart is facing customer dissatisfaction due to inconsistent delivery performance. Some key clients have not renewed contracts citing service issues. The business wants to:

- Track supply chain performance across different cities and customers
- Compare actual vs target KPIs
- Analyze monthly trends to identify underperforming periods
- Take proactive steps to fix delays or short deliveries

---

## 📊 Ad-hoc Business Questions Answered

1. 📦 What are the monthly **OT%, IF%, and OTIF%** trends compared to targets?  
2. 🏙️ Which **cities** perform better or worse in on-time and in-full delivery?  
3. 📈 Which **customers consistently miss delivery targets**, and by how much?  
4. 🔄 What are the **fill rates (line & volume)** by product and by customer?  
5. 📅 In which **months or quarters** are deliveries falling below target KPIs?  
6. 📦 Which products have **low fulfillment performance**?  

---

## 📌 Key Insights

- 📉 The average **OTIF% is 47.95%**, well below the target of 65.91%. This highlights a serious gap in supply chain execution that must be addressed to maintain client satisfaction.
- 🏙️ Among the 3 cities, **Ahmedabad performs best**, consistently exceeding OT and IF targets, whereas **Surat and Vadodara** fall short — especially in the months of **June and July**, suggesting regional bottlenecks.
- 🧾 Customers like **Coolblue** and **Acclaimed Stores** have some of the **lowest OTIF scores (under 20%)**, requiring urgent investigation and service-level improvements.
- 🚛 The **volume fill rate is strong at ~96.6%**, but **line fill rate hovers around 65.9%**, which suggests that while overall quantities are shipped, individual product lines often miss their target — affecting order completeness.
- 📅 Monthly OT, IF, and OTIF values show a **steady underperformance from March to August**, with **no clear recovery**, suggesting systemic inefficiencies rather than isolated events.
- 📦 Certain products like **AM Butter 100**, **AM Curd 100**, and **AM Biscuits 750** consistently show better fill rates, while others lag — providing a clear path to optimize SKU-level supply planning.

---

## 🧾 Final Conclusion

The dashboard reveals significant underperformance in key delivery metrics, especially in OTIF%. By focusing on low-performing cities and customers, AtliQ Mart can improve service reliability, reduce customer churn, and strengthen supply chain resilience.

Specific attention should be given to:
- Regional delivery operations in **Surat and Vadodara**
- Improving **order-level completeness (Line Fill Rate)**
- Aligning performance with high-volume customers like **Coolblue** and **Elite Mart**
